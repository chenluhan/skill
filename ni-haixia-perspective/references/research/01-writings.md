## Systematic Works / Course Corpus

### Tian Ji

- Source: Hantang copyright statement mirrored at https://hantang.org.cn/nihaixia/1.html
- Confidence: high for the statement's content because it attributes the text to Ni's original `hantang.com/chinese/ch_Articles/copyright4.htm`.
- Findings:
  - `Tian Ji` was described as a work created in 1994.
  - It combined I Ching / Ziwei Doushu style destiny philosophy with geographic / feng-shui reasoning.
  - It consisted of 24 DVDs, a fortune-telling program, and two books: `人间道` and `天机道与地脉道`.
- Inference:
  - Ni's system did not treat medicine as a narrow technical craft. He framed medicine, cosmology, time, and place as one pattern-recognition system.

### Ren Ji

- Source: Hantang copyright statement mirrored at https://hantang.org.cn/nihaixia/1.html
- Confidence: high for corpus structure.
- Findings:
  - `Ren Ji` was completed on `2007-07-30`.
  - It was framed as five classics: acupuncture, Huangdi Neijing, Shennong Bencao Jing, Shanghan Lun, and Jinkui Yaolue.
  - The statement lists DVD/book structure: acupuncture, Neijing, Bencao, Shanghan, Jinkui.
- Secondary corroboration:
  - Ni's China support biography also describes `Ren Ji` as five parts and says he taught the course in Taiwan in 2005, flying back periodically from the U.S. to record DVDs.
- Inference:
  - His curriculum is sequential: technique and body map first, theory of physiology next, pharmacology next, then acute pattern change and miscellaneous/chronic disease.

## Recurring Concepts

### Health Before Disease Name

- Source: Hantang `Good Health Quiz`, https://www.hantang.com/english/en_Articles/GOODHEALTHQUIZ.htm
- Confidence: high, institutional page.
- Finding:
  - The page argues that lab-normal results do not necessarily mean health and uses practical daily-function markers: daily bowel rhythm, appetite, urination, sleep, warm hands/feet with cool face, sweating, morning yang sign, pain level, and energy.
- Cross-domain recurrence:
  - The phone diagnosis form asks the same categories: appetite, sleep, urine, bowel, AM yang, four limbs, thirst, sweat, pains.
  - The Liang Dong transcript uses similar markers while discussing heart disease: sleep, chest pain, bowel, cold limbs, numbness, pain.
- Model candidate:
  - `健康标准先于病名`: judge health by stable daily functions and early symptoms, not by disease labels or delayed lab confirmation.

### Symptom-Pattern Rather Than Disease-Name Treatment

- Source: Liang Dong transcript repost and Hantang support course summaries.
- Confidence: medium; transcript is a repost but long and consistent with his institutional pages.
- Finding:
  - Ni repeatedly contrasts Western disease names with TCM symptom/pattern discrimination.
  - In the transcript, he says he does not prescribe by the Western disease name but by the patient's symptoms.
- Model candidate:
  - `以症统病`: disease names are late abstractions; concrete symptom constellation is the operating interface.

### Classical Formula Orthodoxy

- Source: Hantang support Shanghan Lun course summary, Hantang copyright statement, memorial site.
- Confidence: medium for interpretation, high for corpus.
- Findings:
  - The course summary centers six-channel differentiation and formula-pattern matching.
  - The biography and memorial frame him as a modern defender of classical prescriptions (`经方`).
- Inference:
  - He sees Zhang Zhongjing / Shanghan-Jinkui not as historical reference, but as a living operating system for clinical reasoning.

### Natural Food / Anti-Industrial Intervention

- Source: Hantang English diet and patient guideline pages.
- Confidence: high for his institutional stance; medical validity not assumed.
- Findings:
  - Strong preference for natural foods and strong suspicion of processed foods, refined sugar, dairy, coffee, supplements, and some modern medications.
- Safety note:
  - Some pages advise stopping medications or supplements. The generated skill must never convert this into user instructions. It may only treat it as part of Ni's worldview and must redirect users to licensed clinicians for medication decisions.

## Candidate Mental Models From Writings

1. `健康六/九标准`: health is observable in ordinary daily functions before it appears in lab reports.
2. `症状是系统反馈`: symptoms are not nuisances to suppress; they are the body's readable diagnostic signals.
3. `经典是操作系统`: ancient medical classics are not citations for authority; they are executable rule systems.
4. `人-天-地同构`: human physiology, seasonal/time patterns, and environment are one pattern field.
5. `少即是正`: remove artificial interference first: processed food, industrial supplementation, excessive intervention.
