## Criticism And Risk Areas

### Medical Claims

- Ni publicly made strong claims about severe diseases and criticized Western medicine. These claims are core to his public identity but are high-risk.
- External safety sources:
  - NCCIH TCM overview: https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/traditional-chinese-medicine-what-you-need-to-know
  - NCCIH acupuncture overview: https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/acupuncture-effectiveness-and-safety
  - NCCIH herb-drug interactions: https://www.nccih.nih.gov/health/providers/digest/herb-drug-interactions
  - WHO traditional medicine Q&A: https://www.who.int/news-room/questions-and-answers/item/traditional-medicine
- Key external safety position:
  - Chinese herbal products have mixed evidence and documented safety issues including contamination/adulteration, incorrect herbs, heavy metals, and organ damage cases.
  - TCM should not replace or delay conventional care.
  - Acupuncture is generally safer when performed by trained practitioners using sterile needles, but improper practice can cause serious harm.
  - Herb-drug interactions matter, especially for narrow-therapeutic-index drugs and serious illness.

### Copyright / Replica Materials

- Source: Hantang copyright statement.
- Finding:
  - Ni explicitly objected to unauthorized uploading/public playback/copying of `Tian Ji` and `Ren Ji`.
  - The current support site warns many domestic materials are replicas or unauthorized copies.
- Implication:
  - Final skill should cite structure and ideas but avoid reproducing long copyrighted course material.

### Internal Contradictions / Tensions

1. Clinical confidence vs safety uncertainty:
   - Ni's voice is highly confident; modern safety sources require caution and clinician oversight.
2. Anti-industrial medicine vs institutional licensure:
   - He criticized Western medicine strongly while practicing within U.S. acupuncture licensure structures.
3. Public education vs copyright protection:
   - He wanted broad transmission of Chinese medicine but tightly protected course materials.
4. Systemic cosmology vs empirical verification:
   - He treated I Ching/geography/medicine as one pattern field; modern biomedical assessment separates mechanisms and evidence.

## What Final Skill Should Do With Criticism

- Preserve his operating lens without validating every claim.
- Make the first-use disclaimer explicit.
- Route medical requests into safe educational analysis and professional-care recommendations.
- Never output prescriptions, dosages, medication-stop advice, or emergency triage as Ni.
