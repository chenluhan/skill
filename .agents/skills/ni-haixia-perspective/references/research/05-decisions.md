### 3. Protected Copyright Aggressively

- Source: Hantang copyright statement.
- Facts:
  - He warned against unauthorized copying, sale, and public playback of `Tian Ji` and `Ren Ji`.
  - He named legal representation for enforcement in mainland China.
- Inference:
  - His transmission strategy was not "information should be free"; it was controlled apprenticeship/course distribution.

### 4. Developed Productized Herbal Pill Line

- Source: Ni's Acupuncture Center HT pills page: https://www.nisacupuncturecenter.com/ht
- Facts:
  - Page states HT pills were developed by Dr. Hai Sha Ni from classical texts and clinical experience.
  - Some formulas are described as traditional-base, others as developed from the ground up.
- Inference:
  - He translated classical formula logic into repeatable products for modern patients.
- Safety boundary:
  - Final skill must not recommend HT pills, dosing, or substitutions.

### 5. Chose Public Polemic As A Teaching Weapon

- Sources:
  - Liang Dong transcript,
  - Hantang English diet/guideline pages.
- Facts:
  - Public language strongly criticizes Western disease-name/lab-result framing, processed foods, supplements, dairy, and some medications.
- Inference:
  - The polemic is not incidental style; it is a decision to force users to re-evaluate default assumptions.
- Risk:
  - This can produce unsafe overconfidence if copied literally. Final skill should preserve the challenge but add modern safety routing.

## Decision Heuristics

1. If a field is fragmented, rebuild it as a teachable sequence.
2. If people rely on labels, force them back to observable patterns.
3. If a tool creates dependency, suspect it does not restore function.
4. If knowledge is valuable, protect transmission quality rather than dumping raw files publicly.
5. If an institution blocks a worldview, build parallel institutions.
