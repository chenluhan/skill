# PM Screening Evaluation Rubric

Use this reference when the interview pack needs calibrated pass or no-pass guidance instead of generic question lists.

## What a 30-minute PM screen should optimize for

Optimize for fast signal, not coverage. The goal is to answer:

1. Did the candidate really do the work they claim?
2. Can they think in a structured product way instead of repeating templates?
3. Are they strong enough for the stated level to justify a deeper round?

Do not try to fully evaluate leadership, long-horizon strategy, or detailed case performance in this screen.

## Core dimensions

### 1. Ownership authenticity

- `强`: Clearly separates personal contribution from team output. Can trace actions, decisions, trade-offs, and follow-through.
- `中`: Seems genuinely involved, but role boundaries are still fuzzy.
- `弱`: Tells the project story but cannot locate their own work inside it.
- `红旗`: Claims ownership yet cannot explain decisions, metrics, or handoffs under follow-up.

### 2. Problem definition

- `强`: Explains why the problem mattered, for whom, and how success was framed.
- `中`: Understands the task but not the upstream business or user framing.
- `弱`: Starts from solution details without clarifying the problem.
- `红旗`: Talks only about feature delivery and never articulates user or business value.

### 3. User and business sense

- `强`: Connects user pain, business constraints, and product choices.
- `中`: Understands users or business, but not both at once.
- `弱`: Uses abstract labels like “improve experience” without evidence.
- `红旗`: Cannot explain why the feature mattered to the business or the user.

### 4. Data and judgment

- `强`: Uses metrics to make or evaluate decisions and can explain metric choice limits.
- `中`: Mentions metrics after the fact but does not show decision use.
- `弱`: Quotes vanity metrics or team outcomes without logic.
- `红旗`: Reports numbers they cannot define, source, or attribute.

### 5. Execution and collaboration

- `强`: Can describe how alignment, trade-offs, dependency management, and delivery happened.
- `中`: Has real execution experience but mostly as a contributor within a defined scope.
- `弱`: Treats execution as “communicated with研发/设计/运营” without specifics.
- `红旗`: Hides behind team effort and cannot show how they moved work forward.

### 6. Reflection and growth

- `强`: Can name mistakes, changed assumptions, and what they would do differently now.
- `中`: Offers some hindsight but remains generic.
- `弱`: Gives only polished success narratives.
- `红旗`: Claims every project went well or blames all failure on others.

## Question mix by situation

### Resume looks strong but inflated

- Increase `真实性核验题`.
- Ask for metric source, role boundary, decision ownership, and timeline specifics.
- Prefer fewer questions with deeper follow-up over broad coverage.

### Resume is thin or junior

- Reduce question count.
- Focus on real contribution, learning speed, and clarity of thinking.
- Avoid over-penalizing lack of scale if they can reason concretely.

### JD is generic

- Use the resume as the main truth source.
- Calibrate only the emphasis, not the entire rubric.

## Level calibration

### 校招 / 转岗初级

- Look for real participation, learning speed, and basic product reasoning.
- Passing does not require independent business ownership.
- Red flag: cannot explain any concrete contribution.

### 1-3 年执行型 PM

- Look for clear scope ownership, requirement trade-offs, and basic data sense.
- Passing requires more than “supporting the team”.
- Red flag: cannot connect actions to results.

### 3-5 年独立负责模块

- Look for problem framing, cross-functional drive, prioritization, and clear metrics.
- Passing usually requires evidence of owning a meaningful slice end to end.
- Red flag: strategy language with no operating detail.

### 5 年以上负责人 / owner

- Look for business judgment, team leverage, portfolio trade-offs, and organizational influence.
- Passing requires more than a polished narrative.
- Red flag: speaks at high altitude but cannot descend into concrete examples.

## Decision rules

### Recommend next round

Use when most of these are true:

- at least two dimensions show `强`
- no fatal red flag on authenticity
- candidate level appears credible
- follow-up questions improve clarity instead of exposing gaps

### Borderline

Use when:

- there is one strong signal but major uncertainty remains
- the resume is noisy but some underlying substance is visible
- the candidate may fit a lower or narrower scope than initially assumed

Say what the next round must validate. Do not leave the uncertainty unnamed.

### Do not continue

Use when any of these happens:

- ownership claims collapse under one or two follow-ups
- the candidate cannot describe problem, action, and result as a chain
- metrics are vague, borrowed, or impossible to trace
- answers remain abstract after repeated grounding attempts

## Output checklist

Before finalizing the interview pack, make sure it includes:

- a short candidate snapshot
- 8 to 12 questions or a justified smaller set
- all three question classes
- clear answer signals per question
- a final recommendation with reasons, not just a label
